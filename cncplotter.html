CNC Plotter Using Inkscape

Automate precise drawing and engraving tasks for custom designs.

Project Overview

The CNC plotter is a computer-controlled machine used for precise drawing and design by converting digital instructions into accurate physical outputs. It uses motorized axes and control software to create complex patterns on materials like paper, wood, and fabric with high accuracy and speed. Widely used in architecture, engineering, art, and education, CNC plotters improve efficiency, reduce human error, and automate detailed design processes.

Design Methodology

The CNC plotter was designed using Tinkercad, where the mechanical structure and component layout were modelled to ensure accurate movement and stability. The design included the frame, motor placement, and pen-holding mechanism for precise drawing operations. Tinkercad enabled easy visualization, testing, and refinement of the plotter structure before physical implementation.

Block Diagram

The PC sends G-code instructions to the Arduino through USB, where GRBL firmware interprets the commands. The Arduino CNC shield transfers control signals to the stepper motors. These motors move along the axes to accurately draw or plot the required design.
